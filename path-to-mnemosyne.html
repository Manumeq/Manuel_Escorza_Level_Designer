Summary

Path to Mnemosyne is a narrative puzzle exploration game following a young girl navigating fragmented memories within a surreal, kaleidoscopic space.

Achievements

◉ Delivered several narrative puzzles in both 2D and 3D, integrating them directly into Unity

◉ Owned multiple prototypes and early exploration concepts stemming from documentation of the game design team, resulting in strong direction.
‍
◉ Integrated narrative ambience and story into puzzles UI and style
‍
◉ Coded UI elements, logic and menus for additional projects in the studio


Blending narrative and puzzle design
‍

I worked as a puzzle level designer, prototyping and implementing mechanics that supported both gameplay progression and the emotional pacing of the story.My primary focus was the spherical puzzle level, which required a robust physics-driven prototype built directly in Unity.

I developed and iterated on the mechanic in close collaboration with the lead programmer, ultimately delivering a 3D puzzle that was projected onto a 2D surface within the world. Balancing spatial logic, technical constraints, and player readability was key, especially since the interaction had to feel intuitive despite the dimensional shift.I also designed and implemented a laser-beam puzzle centered around lens manipulation.

While technically simpler, this puzzle required careful attention to clarity and visual flow so players could engage with the mechanic without losing focus on the narrative. I ensured the space remained readable and cohesive with the art direction, supporting both immersion and player psychology.
